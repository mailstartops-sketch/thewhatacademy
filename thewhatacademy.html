Program 01 — Deep Dive

Corporate Readiness for Campus

Bridging the gap between academic preparation and workplace reality.

The Problem This Solves

Most campus placements place technically competent graduates into organisations — and then wonder why the first year is so painful for everyone involved. Students know their subject. They don't know how organisations actually work.

They haven't been taught what accountability really looks like. They don't understand why communication matters more than capability in the first 90 days. They've never had to navigate a hierarchy, manage up, or deal with ambiguity that has no answer key. This program fills that gap before it becomes a performance issue.

What the Program Covers

  • How organisations actually work — structure, hierarchy, and how decisions get made
  • What accountability means in a professional context (beyond "meeting deadlines")
  • Communication that gets taken seriously: written, verbal, and in meetings
  • Managing up — how to work with managers who have different styles and pressures
  • The first 90 days: what to prioritise, what to avoid, what to prove
  • How to navigate ambiguity when there's no rubric
  • Professional presence and credibility — what builds it, what destroys it

Who This Is For

Colleges and universities who want to differentiate their placement outcomes. Corporate HR teams who want to reduce the cost of onboarding underperformance. The audience is final-year students or recent graduates across any discipline.

Format and Delivery

A 2-day intensive workshop, delivered on-campus or virtually. Cohorts of 30–120 students. High interaction, no slide-reading — Pradeep runs this as a conversation, not a lecture. Real scenarios, real questions, real answers.

Program 02 — Deep Dive

What Corporates Need to Know About New-Age Employees

Understanding a workforce that has fundamentally changed.

The Problem This Solves

The workforce born between 1995 and 2010 communicates differently, processes feedback differently, and defines meaningful work differently than every generation before them. And most organisations are still trying to manage them with approaches that worked in 2005.

The result: high attrition, disengagement that looks like attitude problems, and a widening gap between what organisations offer and what this workforce actually needs. The cost is real — in time, in money, and in institutional knowledge that walks out the door.

This program doesn't validate entitlement. It builds understanding — so that HR heads and senior managers can lead this workforce more effectively, retain the talent worth keeping, and stop misreading what's actually happening.

What the Program Covers

  • How the new-age employee thinks about work, hierarchy, and loyalty
  • Why they leave — what the exit surveys don't capture
  • The difference between entitlement and unmet clarity (it matters)
  • How to give feedback they'll actually receive
  • What psychological safety means operationally, not philosophically
  • Retention: what works, what's performative, and what costs nothing
  • Managing the tension between accountability and autonomy

Who This Is For

HR heads, L&D leaders, and senior managers in mid-size and large organisations who are hiring, managing, and trying to retain Gen Z and younger millennial employees. The audience is people leaders — not the employees themselves.

Program 03 — Deep Dive

What It Takes to Be a Founder

The operating reality most founder education won't tell you about.

The Problem This Solves

Most founder education is taught by people who've either never built a business, or who've had such a specific experience that it doesn't generalise. It focuses on funding rounds, product-market fit, and pitch decks — and almost completely ignores what it actually feels like to build something when you don't know if it'll work.

This cohort is different. It's not about validation frameworks or investor relations. It's about the operating reality — the decisions that feel impossible, the loneliness that nobody warns you about, the things that will haunt you at 2am if you're not prepared for them.

What the Cohort Covers

  • What you're actually signing up for — the psychological reality of founding
  • How to know if your idea is a business or a hobby (the honest version)
  • First hires: what to look for, what to avoid, when to make the call
  • Money: what founder finance actually looks like day-to-day
  • How to sell when you're not a salesperson — and why you have to anyway
  • What "early customers" really teach you (if you're paying attention)
  • When to pivot, when to persist, and how to tell the difference
  • The things most founders wish someone had told them before they started

Format

6 weeks, 2 sessions per week (evenings, online), plus one full in-person day in Bengaluru. Cohort size is 15–20 — small by design, so that real conversation is possible. No lecture format. Every session is built around questions, scenarios, and honest discussion.

Who Should Apply

Aspiring founders who are 6–24 months from starting something. People who are serious — not just curious. You don't need to have a business yet, but you should be genuinely considering it. This cohort is not for passive learning.

Program 04 — Deep Dive

Relevance — Leadership Program

How mid-career leaders stay indispensable as everything changes around them.

The Question Nobody Is Asking Out Loud

Most organisations aren't openly discussing which of their senior managers are becoming irrelevant. But the conversation is happening in boardrooms and HR reviews every quarter. Mid-career leaders — people with 10, 15, 20 years of experience — are suddenly finding that what made them successful in the past isn't automatically going to keep them relevant going forward.

AI is changing what knowledge work looks like. Teams that used to require deep expertise now require something else: judgment, clarity, and the ability to navigate uncertainty. The leaders who thrive in the next decade won't be the most experienced — they'll be the most relevant.

This program answers the question: what does relevant leadership look like now?

What the Program Will Cover

  • What relevance actually means in a leadership context — it's not what most people think
  • How AI is changing the value of experience, expertise, and institutional knowledge
  • The leadership skills that compound in value as everything else gets automated
  • How to lead teams that are more technically capable than you are in some areas
  • Presence and influence — what it looks like in 2026 and beyond
  • How to avoid the trap of leading the way you were led

Format and Connection to the Podcast

The Relevance Leadership Program is designed as a workshop series for corporate teams, linked to the Relevance podcast hosted by Pradeep. Programme participants will have direct access to podcast guests and live conversation sessions. Details being finalised for Q3 2026 launch.
